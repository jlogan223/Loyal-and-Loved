Introduction

Planning a holiday with your dog should not feel like a compromise. No more scanning listings for the tiny "pets welcome" footnote, only to arrive and find the cottage has cream carpets, no garden, and a two-page list of rooms your dog cannot enter. The UK has a genuinely excellent selection of dog-friendly holiday cottages, and the best ones are designed around your dog's needs, not just tolerating them.

This guide compares the major UK cottage booking providers, explains what separates a truly dog-friendly property from one that merely allows pets, and links to our four in-depth regional guides covering Scotland, the Lake District, Cornwall, and Wales.

Quick Answer

Sykes Holiday Cottages has the largest selection (13,700+ pet-friendly properties) with excellent filters for enclosed gardens and number of dogs. Snaptrip covers 36,000+ properties with competitive last-minute pricing. For the best dog-friendly experience, prioritise cottages with a fully fenced garden, hard floors, and walks from the door. Expect to pay £0-£50 per dog per stay as a pet supplement.

What Makes a Cottage Genuinely Dog-Friendly?

There is a spectrum between "pets allowed" and "built for dogs." When searching for a cottage, these are the features that make the difference between a relaxing holiday and a stressful one.

Enclosed or fenced garden. This is the single most important feature. A secure garden means your dog can stretch their legs, have a sniff about, and do their business without you needing to get fully dressed and find a lead at 6am. Look for descriptions mentioning "fully enclosed," "fenced," or "secure garden." Some providers like Sykes let you filter specifically for this.

Hard floors or washable surfaces. Muddy paws and cream carpets do not mix. The best dog-friendly cottages have stone, tile, or wood flooring in the main living areas, or at the very least, a boot room or utility area where you can clean up before entering the main house.

Dog wash or boot room. A dedicated space with hot water for hosing down a muddy dog is a genuine luxury. Some premium cottages now include outdoor dog showers or purpose-built wash stations.

Walks from the door. The best cottages for dogs are the ones where you step outside and you are already on a footpath, beach, or open countryside. No car journey needed to find a decent walk.

Dog-friendly pubs and cafes nearby. A holiday is not just about the cottage. Check whether nearby pubs, cafes, and restaurants welcome dogs, particularly in rural areas where options may be limited.

Clear pet policy. A good listing tells you exactly what to expect: how many dogs are welcome, which rooms are off-limits (if any), whether dogs are allowed on furniture, and whether there is a pet supplement charge. Vague listings often mean the owner has not thought it through.

Sykes Holiday Cottages

Sykes Holiday Cottages is one of the UK's largest cottage rental platforms, with over 13,700 pet-friendly properties across England, Scotland, Wales, Ireland, and Northern Ireland. Their search filters are the best in the industry for dog owners: you can filter by number of pets (1-6), enclosed gardens, ground-floor bedrooms (useful for older dogs), and even hot tubs if you fancy unwinding after a long walk.

Properties range from cosy one-bedroom bolthole cottages to large farmhouses sleeping 20+. Pet supplements vary by property, with many welcoming dogs free of charge and others charging £20-£50 per dog per stay. Each listing clearly states the pet policy, number of dogs allowed, and whether the garden is enclosed.

Sykes also have a flexible cancellation policy on many properties, which is worth checking before booking, particularly if your plans might change.

Snaptrip

Snaptrip aggregates over 36,000 properties from professional holiday letting partners across the UK. Their dog-friendly collection covers cottages, lodges, apartments, and country houses, with properties available in popular dog-walking regions from Devon to the Scottish Highlands.

Where Snaptrip excels is on last-minute availability and price comparison. Because they work with multiple letting agencies, you can sometimes find the same property listed at different prices and grab a deal. Their membership programme offers additional discounts for regular bookers.

Pet fees are displayed on each listing, so there are no surprises at checkout. Properties range from budget countryside retreats to luxury coastal homes.

Cottages.com

Cottages.com is one of the most established names in UK holiday lettings, part of the Awaze group (which also includes Hoseasons). They have a solid selection of dog-friendly properties with clear filtering by region, number of bedrooms, and pet policies.

Their properties tend to sit in the mid-range to premium bracket, with well-maintained cottages in popular holiday regions. Many properties come with enclosed gardens and are close to walking routes. They also offer lodges and parks in addition to standalone cottages.

Canine Cottages

Canine Cottages is the specialist in this space. As the name suggests, every single property on their platform is dog-friendly, and many include dog welcome packs with treats, towels, and local walk recommendations.

Their collection is smaller than Sykes or Snaptrip, but quality is consistently high. Every property is chosen specifically for suitability with dogs, so you are less likely to arrive and find the "dog-friendly" label was an afterthought. They also publish a dog-friendly travel blog with walk recommendations, pub guides, and beach reviews across the UK.

Pet Travel Checklist

Before you set off, make sure you have packed everything your dog needs. It is easy to forget something when you are focused on your own packing.

Category What to bring Why it matters
Safety Dog seatbelt or travel crate, microchip details, up-to-date ID tag with holiday address Legal requirement: dogs must wear a collar and tag with owner's name and address in public. Update the tag with your holiday address.
Health Vet records, vaccination certificate, any medication, flea/tick treatment Tick risk is higher in rural areas (especially Scotland, Lake District, Wales). Apply treatment before travel if due.
Comfort Dog bed or blanket, favourite toy, food and water bowls Familiar items reduce stress in a new environment. Most cottages do not provide dog beds.
Walking Lead (standard and long line), harness, poo bags, portable water bottle Many beaches and nature reserves require dogs on leads during nesting season (March-July).
Clean-up Old towels, dog wipes, spare blanket for car boot Muddy walks are guaranteed in the UK. Cottages may charge for cleaning muddy furnishings.
Food Enough food for the full stay plus one extra day, treats Rural areas may not stock your dog's usual food. Bring enough to avoid an upset stomach from a sudden diet change.

For flea and tick treatments and other travel health supplies, you can order from an online pet pharmacy in advance and have them delivered before your trip. If you are tracking your dog's location on walks in unfamiliar areas, see our GPS tracker comparison.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many dogs can I take to a holiday cottage?

Most providers allow 1-2 dogs as standard, with some properties accepting up to 6. Sykes Holiday Cottages lets you filter by number of pets (1-6). Always check the individual property listing as limits vary by owner, not just provider.

Do holiday cottages charge extra for dogs?

It varies by provider and property. Some cottages welcome dogs free of charge, while others charge a pet supplement of around £20-£50 per dog per stay. Both Sykes and Snaptrip show pet fees on each listing before you book, so there are no surprises.

What makes a cottage genuinely dog-friendly?

A genuinely dog-friendly cottage goes beyond simply allowing pets. Look for: a fully enclosed or fenced garden, hard floors or washable surfaces in main rooms, a dog wash or boot room, walks accessible from the front door, nearby dog-friendly pubs and beaches, and clear information about any restricted areas within the property. See our full breakdown above.

Do I need to bring my own dog bed and bowls?

Most cottages do not provide dog equipment, so plan to bring your dog's bed, food and water bowls, leads, towels, and any medication. Some specialist providers like Canine Cottages include dog welcome packs with treats and towels. Check the property listing for what is provided.

Can I leave my dog alone in the cottage?

Most cottage owners ask that dogs are not left unattended in the property, primarily to avoid damage and barking complaints from neighbours. If you need to visit a dog-free attraction, plan ahead by booking a local dog-sitting service or choosing attractions that welcome dogs.

Key Takeaway

The UK has thousands of genuinely dog-friendly holiday cottages, and the best booking platforms make it easy to filter for what matters most: enclosed gardens, number of dogs allowed, and proximity to great walks. Sykes Holiday Cottages offers the largest selection with the best filters. Snaptrip is strongest for last-minute deals and price comparison. Canine Cottages is the quality-first specialist. Whichever you choose, prioritise a fenced garden and walks from the door, and use our travel checklist to make sure you do not forget anything.
